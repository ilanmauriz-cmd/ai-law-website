A proposed law that protects people from unfair, unsafe, and unaccountable artificial intelligence while still allowing innovation to grow.

What AI Problem Does This Law Solve?

Artificial intelligence is becoming more powerful every year, but it is not always fair or safe. Some systems can be biased, meaning they may make less accurate or unfair decisions for certain groups of people. In addition, many AI systems are released too quickly without proper testing, which can lead to serious harm in areas such as healthcare, education, hiring, and public safety.

Main issue: AI is advancing faster than the laws meant to protect people from its mistakes.

Main Rules of the Law

  • Fairness testing: AI must be tested on diverse populations to ensure it performs equally and fairly across different groups before being used in important decisions.
  • Safety approval: High-risk AI must pass official safety reviews.
  • Human oversight: A person must review major AI decisions before action is taken.
  • Transparency: Companies must clearly explain how the AI works, what data it uses, and any potential risks associated with it.
  • Accountability: If AI causes serious harm due to negligence, the company can be held responsible.

Who Benefits?

  • Students and workers
  • Patients and families
  • Communities affected by unfair technology
  • Businesses that want people to trust AI
  • Society as a whole

Possible Criticisms

  • Some people may say it slows down innovation.
  • Companies may say the rules are too expensive.
  • Others may argue that AI changes too fast to regulate easily.

Why Voters Should Support It

Voters should support this law because it protects people while still allowing innovation to grow. It creates clear and reasonable rules that ensure AI is developed responsibly. By reducing unfair treatment, preventing harmful mistakes, and holding companies accountable, this law builds trust in technology and ensures it benefits everyone.

Simple message: Safer AI. Fairer decisions. Stronger protection.
